What's Working

Bitcoin spot ETFs brought institutional access. Stablecoins are processing trillions in transfers. Regulatory frameworks are emerging (GENIUS Act signed). Advisor access has doubled since 2023.

⚠️

What's Concerning

Extreme volatility persists. Fraud losses hit a record $11.4B in 2025 — adults over 60 lost $4.4B alone, making them the most targeted demographic. Political entanglement with crypto creates regulatory uncertainty.

🧭

Your Role

Clients are making crypto decisions without you. The advisors who engage — even at a basic literacy level — catch scams, improve risk management, and protect AUM that would otherwise walk out the door.

How We Got Here (2008–2026)

A condensed timeline of the events that shape how advisors should think about crypto today.

  • 2008
    Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the Bitcoin whitepaper
  • 2009
    Bitcoin network launches; genesis block mined January 3
  • 2013–2017
    First exchange collapses (Mt. Gox 2014); ICO boom and bust
  • 2020–2021
    Institutional entry begins; BTC crosses $60K; DeFi and NFT cycles
  • 2022
    Terra/Luna, Celsius, BlockFi, Voyager, and FTX collapse. ~$2T in market cap wiped out.
  • 2024
    SEC approves spot Bitcoin ETFs (January 10); spot Ethereum ETFs follow in July. First major institutional access vehicle.
  • 2025
    Regulatory reset: Paul Atkins confirmed as SEC Chair; SAB 121 rescinded; GENIUS Act signed into law; Strategic Bitcoin Reserve established; Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, Ripple cases dismissed.
  • 2026
    SEC/CFTC joint interpretive release classifies 16 digital assets as non-securities (March). Spot ETFs now exist for BTC, ETH (with staking), SOL, XRP, LTC, HBAR, DOGE.
Regulatory Landscape

Regulatory Snapshot (as of April 2026)

What advisors need to know right now about the evolving crypto regulatory framework.

Agency Leadership

SEC Chair: Paul Atkins

Confirmed April 2025. Replaced Gary Gensler. Shifted SEC posture from enforcement-first to rulemaking-first.

Agency Leadership

CFTC Chair: Michael Selig

Confirmed December 2025. Overseeing expanded CFTC role in digital commodity spot markets.

Signed Into Law

SAB 122 (January 2025)

Rescinded SAB 121 — crypto custody no longer requires balance-sheet liability treatment. This is why banks can now custody digital assets.

Signed Into Law

GENIUS Act (July 18, 2025)

First federal digital asset law. Regulates payment stablecoins. Effective ~November 2026.

Pending

CLARITY Act

Passed House July 2025, stalled in Senate. Would establish CFTC jurisdiction over digital commodity spot markets. Not yet law.

Joint Release

SEC/CFTC Interpretive Release (March 2026)

Classified 16 named digital assets (incl. BTC, ETH, XRP, SOL) as non-securities. Establishes current token taxonomy.

Executive Order

Strategic Bitcoin Reserve (March 2025)

Government holds ~200,000 BTC from forfeitures; will not sell. Established via EO March 6, 2025.

Enforcement Reset

Major Cases Dismissed

Coinbase (Feb 2025), Kraken (Mar 2025), Binance (May 2025), Ripple (Aug 2025). Fraud cases continue; regulatory overreach cases dropped.

Tax Considerations

Form 1099-DA is new for the 2025 tax year. Brokers issued first 1099-DAs in February 2026 reporting gross proceeds only. Cost basis reporting becomes mandatory for the 2026 tax year (forms issued 2027) on assets acquired January 2026 or later at the same broker.

Wash sale rules do not currently apply to directly held crypto. Because crypto is classified as property rather than securities, IRC §1091 does not apply. Clients can sell at a loss and immediately repurchase. This is a legitimate tax-loss harvesting tool that may not survive — bipartisan proposals to extend wash sale rules to digital assets are active in Congress.

Spot crypto ETFs are treated as securities for tax purposes. Wash sale rules DO apply to ETF shares. Advisors should distinguish between direct crypto holdings and ETF exposure when modeling client tax scenarios.

Scam Protection

Red Flag Reference

Keep this section bookmarked. When a client brings up a crypto "opportunity," run through these before anything else.

🚨 Language Red Flags

  • 🔴 "Guaranteed returns" — no legitimate investment guarantees anything
  • 🔴 "Risk-free" — crypto is inherently volatile; this is a lie
  • 🔴 "Limited time offer" — pressure tactics; legitimate opportunities don't expire in 24 hours
  • 🔴 "Double your money" — classic scam promise
  • 🔴 "Only insiders know about this" — if it were real, they wouldn't be telling strangers
  • 🔴 "This is the next Bitcoin" — almost all alternative tokens fail
  • 🔴 "Celebrity X endorsed this" — fake endorsements are rampant; verify independently

⚠️ Channel Red Flags

  • 🔴 Unsolicited DM via Telegram, WhatsApp, or Discord — scammers' preferred method
  • 🔴 Social media comment or reply — impersonation is trivial on these platforms
  • 🔴 Dating app match — the "pig butchering" romance scam vector
  • 🔴 A "friend" the client has never met in person
  • 🔴 Unsolicited email with investment opportunity
  • 🟡 YouTube/TikTok influencer — many are paid promoters, not advisors

🎭 What Scammers Say About Advisors

  • 💬 "What are their credentials?"
  • 💬 "They know old money — what do they know about new money?"
  • 💬 "They just want fees; that's why they don't care about crypto."
  • 💬 "Are they protecting you or preventing you?"
  • 💬 "Do they even have Telegram?"

These lines are designed to isolate clients from trusted advisors. Your literacy is your best counter-argument.

🕵️ Common Scam Types

  • 💔 Pig Butchering: Fake romance → trust-building → "investment" → funds drained over time
  • 🏦 Fake Exchange: Convincing website, shows fake gains, blocks withdrawals
  • 🎭 Impersonation: Pretends to be Coinbase support, a celebrity, or a known project
  • 🏃 Rug Pull: Legitimate-looking project, team disappears with funds
  • 📧 Phishing: Fake emails or sites that capture login credentials
  • 📈 Pump & Dump: Hype a worthless token, sell high, leave buyers holding nothing
Quick Client Checklist — Run Through This Before Any Crypto Decision
Did someone approach you, or did you find them?
Are you being asked to act quickly?
Were you asked to move funds to a new wallet or exchange?
Are returns described as "guaranteed" or "risk-free"?
Is the communication happening on Telegram, WhatsApp, or Discord?
Did a romantic interest introduce you to this opportunity?
Are you being asked to keep this secret from family or your advisor?

2+ "Yes" answers = HIGH probability of scam. Pause and investigate.
